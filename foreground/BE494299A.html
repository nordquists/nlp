  PERFECTIONNEMENTS RELATIFS AUX; RAQUETTES DE TENNIS DE TABLE. 



   Cette invention se rapporte aux raquettes ou palettes de tennis de table et elle a pour objet de fournir une raquette ou palette de tennis de tabler qui soit d'un meilleur équilibre dans -la main. 



   Selon l'invention, la partie constituant la poignée   dune   raquet- te de tennis de table est équilibrée par la disposition   d'un   petit poids lourd à   l'intérieur   de la partie formant poignée et près de la base de celle- ci, de façon à rendre le centre de gravité de la raquette voisin de l'extré- mité de la poignée. 



   De façon que l'invention puisse être comprise clairement et aisé- ment mise en   oeuvre,   elle sera maintenant, décrite plus complètement avec ré- férence au dessin ci-annexé et à l'aide de ce dernier, dans   lequel-.'

  IMPROVEMENTS RELATING TO; TABLE TENNIS RACKETS.



   This invention relates to table tennis rackets or paddles and it is an object of the invention to provide a table tennis racket or paddle which is better balanced in the hand.



   According to the invention, the part constituting the grip of a table tennis racquet is balanced by the arrangement of a small heavy weight inside the part forming the grip and near the base thereof, so to make the center of gravity of the racket close to the end of the grip.



   So that the invention may be clearly understood and easily practiced, it will now be described more fully with reference to and with the aid of the accompanying drawing, in which -.
Figure 1 is a plan view of a table tennis racket made according to the invention.



   Figure 2 is an elevational view corresponding to that of Figure 1.



     FIG. 3 is an elbow view. on a larger scale, the grip of the racket.



   If we refer to the drawing, we see that the table tennis racket comprises a sheet 1, in one piece. of plywood, formed into a substantially circular striking plate, with a narrow extension 2, which forms part of the. handle 3, An opening 4 is cut near the outer end of the narrow extension 2 and this opening 4 is intended to accommodate a weight of lead 5, preferably one ounce, or the weight may vary, depending on the desire to create a balance in relation to the weight of the racket.

   The weight 5 is held in place by pieces of wood 6, suitably molded, which are each fixed to a face of the narrow extension, to complete the part.ie 3 constituting the grip of the racket.

   The two sides of the dish 1, intended to strike, of the racket are covered with rubber 7 or any other suitable material.



   The racquet's center of gravity is near the end of the grip portion, where it runs towards the flat of the racquet, and, while with racquets as hitherto manufactured , the balance of the racket in the hand had to suffer from the excessively heavy weight of the dish relative to the grip, a racket made according to the invention has a much improved balance and is, therefore, better in use. wise.
