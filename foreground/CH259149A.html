  Ping pong paddle. The invention relates to a ping-ipong racket.



  In a ping-pong racket according to the. present invention, the paddle that this racket comprises is perforated. It is preferable that the perforated racket bears on at least one of its two faces, several projecting bands made of an elastic material, such as rubber or one of its substitutes;

   also preferably, the handle of the ping-pong racket is provided on at least one of its two sides with a notch close to the paddle; this notch forms a guide and marks a stop position for the thumb of the user.



  It is preferable that the total area of the drills or holes is small compared to that of. the pallet and, although the pallets may be of any material, any shell, for example wood, wood covered with cork, leather or a substitute thereof, or metal, such as aluminum or zinc, it is preferred that said surfaces are rubber or one of its successors.

   In a preferred embodiment and to give the racket a certain rigidity, while leaving it sufficient elasticity or flexibility, the racket com takes a piece of light metal, to which is bonded, over the entire surface and two sides, of rubber or one of its substitutes, the perforations being made in the metal part and maintained, when the part bears, bound to it on both sides, the rubber or its substitute.



  Preferably, the pallets are provided, as has been said above, with several protruding bands, made with an elastic material and preferably with caout chouc, said bands being either flat and arranged perpendicular to each other, in. across the surface, or some other shape. Instead of being flat, these bands can, if we do so. desires, to be formed into the blade of a knife.



  The appended drawing represents, by way of example, an embodiment of the ping-pong rod according to the invention.



       Fig. 1 is a front view thereof, FIG. 2 a front view of the light metal part, without the elastic material linked to this part, and FIG. 3 a section of the racket, on an enlarged scale.



  The racket shown by the drawings comprises a piece 1 of aluminum, in the shape of a ping-pong racket and por both perforations 2 on the. palette. This part 1 forms the body of the racket and, in order to produce the finished object, to part 1 is linked rubber or one of its successors 3.

   In order to maintain the perforations in the finished racket, the elastic material, in the form of rubber or one of its substitutes, is bonded to the aluminum part <B> 1 </B> in a press and -is formed so as to produce protruding bands 4, which, as is; sees, are arranged per- p;

  endicularly to each other, across the surface of the racket, the perforations being held by allowing the rubber to flow through the perforations of the part 1, so as to bond to this part, this being made possible by the presence of dowels or other similar parts arranged. in the press, these dowels having a diameter smaller than that of the perforations 2 of the part 1. It will thus be realized that the perforations of the finished rack have a smaller diameter than the perforations of the part 1.



  The finished racket is provided with a handle 5, formed so as to carry a part 6 having the shape of a groove or. notch, which acts as a comfortable grip for the user's thumb. This groove or notch exists on both sides of the racquet grip ;, so that the latter can be used by a left or right handed player.



  Such a ping-pong racket offers less resistance to the air, when used and, by the fact of the existence of the protruding bands of elastic material on the surface with which one strikes, the combination of perforations and bands provide great accuracy and good tuning when hitting the ping-pong ball. When using the racquet, the perforations allow air to pass through it,

   when it is carried forward to strike the ball, which is reflected in the accuracy of the shock, the ball coming in a straight line against the racquet. In addition, the crossed beams touch the ball, which is at one stroke under the absolute control of the receiver, in order to hit it, direct it and throw it, as he wishes.



  In addition, it will be appreciated that the table tennis racket described has the additional advantage of suppleness or flexibility, in that it is formed of a light metal, such as aluminum. or zinc, and an elastic material. such as rubber, cabbage or one of its substitutes, and that it thus gives increased power to the service of the servant or to the return stroke, with a notably less effort,

       which reduces the fatigue of the player.
