Abstract

The table tennis bat has the axis of the blade (1) at a sharp angle to the axis of the grip (2). When the bat is held in a normal, uncramped position. The ideal hitting zone of the bat lies on an extension of the axis of the lower arm. In one position where the blade passes into the handle a grip step (3) is provided for the index finger forming a form of pistol grip. A further step (4) is provided for the skin piece between the thumb and the index finger. A convex pressure paint (5) is also provided for the thumb. A further pressure point (6) in the form of an extension on the grip side, which can have its size altered by breaking away parts so that the grip fits the hand width.
