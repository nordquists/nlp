Abstract

A table tennis or ping pong ball pick up device 99 consisting of a table tennis bat or paddle which comprises an extending pick up mechanism 38 in the tip of the paddle blade 1. The mechanism uses a coaxially telescopic extendable arm 9 which may be operated by a push button (7, fig.1a) release mechanism (3, fig.1a) which pushes the collector 38 out of the blade via a spring, to allow easier retrieval of balls from the floor. The mechanism includes a pivot 13 between the elongate member and the head of the extension, while a catch part 11 may release or hold the retracted extension in place. A lacuna 12 may trap the ball against the basal part 10 under gravity within the collector head 40. A lid 8 or cap may cover the extending arm when it is contained within the bat, and may be ferromagnetically (18, fig.7) attached.

A TABLE TENNIS DEVICE
Field of the Invention
The present invention relates to a table tennis device, in particular a device for providing easier games of table tennis; more particularly a device providing a means of collecting table tennis balls without bending.
Background
Increasing awareness of the importance of sports and activities is accompanied in many societies by increasing numbers of users with different physical needs.
For example elderly or less able users may find difficulties with even relatively static sports or activities, particularly during learning stages.
In particular table tennis can be an enjoyable activity for many, being relatively static during play. However retrieving the ball can require a degree of movement, bending and the like, that can be off-putting for some users, even where they are fully able.

Summary of the Invention
According to the present invention there is provided a table tennis device comprising a handle and a blade, wherein the blade comprises an extendable ball collector, the ball collector comprising a head, which head is arranged to pivot to engage a ball for collection.
In some embodiments the collector comprises an elongate member, which member supports the head. The member may be substantially rigid.
In some embodiments pivoting is achieved by use of a pivot between the elongate member and the head, which pivot enables the head to pivot axially with reference to an axis described by the member.
The member may be coaxially telescopically arranged to extend from the blade of the bat, for example wherein the blade comprises a compartment within, and the member and head are arranged to locate into this compartment.
The compartment may comprise a lid or cap, which lid or cap is arranged to close over the compartment and may present an unbroken surface to the blade edge when closed in use.
The member in particular, and/or collector in general, may be arranged on a spring biased extending mechanism, such that release of the collector, for example displacement of the cap, may enable the collector to extend automatically.
The head may comprise a ball attachment means, which attachment means may enable the collector to attach the ball to the head at a distance, or automatically.
In some embodiments the pivoting of the head on the pivot enables the attachment means to be operable, or engages the attachment means so as to attach the ball to the head. The pivoting may be automatic when the ball is engaged by the attachment means, or the attachment means may be operated by forcing the head against the ball and engaging the pivot.
For example in use a user may push the head against the ball, pivot the head and engage the attachment means, wherein upon release of the pressure the pivot returns and the ball is attached to the head.
In this way the ball may be lifted by the device, and the ball retrieved without the user bending at any point.
In some embodiments the head may be easily retracted towards the blade for retrieval of the attached ball, for example through a mechanism or through gravity.
In some embodiments the collector, or parts thereof, or mechanism for extending the collector, may be stored within or located on the handle. For example in some embodiments the handle may comprise a release mechanism, such as a button to release the collector, head or member, or a spring extending the head, collector or member, wherein the mechanism may comprise a catch mechanism catching against effect of the spring bias.
In some embodiments the mechanism may not comprise a spring bias, but may be arranged to be actuated, and the head extended from the compartment, by the weight or mass of the head. In this way it may be envisaged that the head may extend from the blade under gravity, and/or may retract into the blade through gravity.
A sprung catch mechanism may be present for placement and displacement of the head itself within the compartment on the blade.
The mechanism for collector extension may thereby comprise a bipartite or multipartite mechanism, such as a release and a catch, arranged at the handle and blade edge respectively.

Detailed Description of Figures
With reference to the figures there is generally shown a table tennis device 99 comprising a handle 2 and a blade 1, wherein the blade 1 comprises an extendable ball collector 38, the ball collector comprising a head 40, which head is arranged to pivot to engage a ball 60 for collection.
In particular reference to the pictured embodiment there is shown an embodiment comprising a coaxially telescopic extendable collector 38 extending from the opposing edge of the frame 4 of the blade 1 from the handle 2, the collector head 40 comprising a part 11 arranged to pivot on a pivot 13.
The device comprises a table tennis bat, which comprises a rigid blade and handle.
The blade 1 is multilayer, comprising two faces 14 with resiliently deformable covering layers, sandwiching an inner body, formed of a wooden perimetric frame 4 with two substantially circular plates facing each other and providing substrate for the covering layers.
The perimetric frame is discontinuous at the handle 2, which extends outwards from the frame at a bottom end at shoulders 15 and having an enlarged section with padded faces 16.
At the distal point of the frame from the handle the frame is also discontinuous with a displaceable segment providing a lid 8 over an internal compartment 39. As shown in Figure 4 the compartment 39 extends between the faces 14 and into the handle 2, which is otherwise solid.
The lid segment comprises the top surface 8 of the collector head 40.
The collector comprises the head 40 and an extending part provided by an elongate member 9 extending coaxially telescopically, and formed in a rigid metal, alloy or thermoplastics.
The proximal end of the collector’s member is anchored in the handle at a spring 17.
The distal end of the collector’s member is the head 40, which is provided by a collecting basal part 10, which extends axially from the member 9 and comprises the pivot 13 to either side adjacent the member 9.
The pivot 13 provides attachment for the other part of the collecting head 40, or the catch part 11, which acts to catch the ball. The top surface of this catch part 11 is provided by the lid 8.
The catch part 11 describes a circular central lacuna 12 in a flat support, which support extends to either side for support of the lid at its top surface 8, where the bottom surface of this, extending sidewards, provides support for two ferromagnetic portions 18.
In use the catch 11 pivots away from coplanar the basal part 10, opening a space between the lacuna 12 and the basal part 10, into which the ball 60 fits to enable it to be grip by the weight of the catch part 11 and enable collecting.
The magnetic portions 18 collaborate with ferromagnetic portions in the frame edge of the blade 1, such that the two keep the collector head 40 in a stored position until needed. The user can throw the blade 1 at the floor to break the ferromagnetic bond and release the collector 38 for use.
In addition or the alternative the device comprises a release mechanism 3, which comprises a slide button 7, sliding in a slot 6 in an indentation 5 on one of the handle faces. This mechanism 3 pushes the collector 38 from the compartment 39 when required, or more particularly breaks the ferromagnetic catch and allows the spring 17 to push the collector 18 out from the compartment 39.
The basal part 10 comprises an inclined lower face/end 19, allowing the basal part 10 to be slid under the ball 60 easily, and the catch part 11 pushed away or opened by contact with the top of the ball 60. Alternatively or additionally the catch part 11 may be opened away from the basal part 10 and placed on top ofthe ball 60 so as to grip the ball between the lacuna 12 and the basal part 10 by gravity’s effect on the catch
11.
