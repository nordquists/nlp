445,374. Table games. ROBERTS BROS. (GLOUCESTER), Ltd., Glevum Works, St. James', Gloucester, and-BUTLER; B., 30, Byron Road, St. Marks, Cheltenham. Dec. 7, 1934, No. 35208. [Class 132 (ii)] In a table tennis game which may be played by persons seated at opposite ends of a table, the players are provided with two-handled bats 1, one pistol-grip handle 2 on either side of the striking surface 3 being grasped in each hand, and the table is fitted with a low playing- net and with safety nets extending first horizontally from the longitudinal edges of the table and then vertically upwards.
