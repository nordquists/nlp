Abstract

455,529. Bats. HALEY, H. H. J., 135, Ross Road, South Norwood, London. Sept. 30, 1935, No. 26949. [Class 132 (ii)] A table-tennis bat is provided with a forward extension E from the handle on to both playing surfaces to provide finger and thumb grips or surfaces, and stop that portion of the player's hand which normally lies on the blade of the bat from coming into contact with the rubber surface of the bat. The extension may be made of sheet cork, thin plywood, metal, parchment, or other suitable material.
