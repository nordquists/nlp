Abstract

648,034. Moulding game bats. HALEX, Ltd., and SNELL, S. W. Nov. 10, 1948, No. 29175. [Class 87 (ii)] [Also in Group XV] A table-tennis or like bat is moulded in one piece from superimposed layers A of sheet material such as creped paper impregnated with thermosetting plastic material and is formed with a face portion A<SP>2</SP> and a hollow tubular handle A<SP>3</SP>, the layers A<SP>1</SP> of sheet material in each circumferential half of the handle being continuous with the layers forming the adjacent half of the thickness of the face portion. The moulding is performed in a heated mould C, C<SP>1</SP>, a mandrel B being inserted between the sheets to form the handle which is afterwards closed by a cap D and may be ridged as at A<SP>8</SP>. The face portion is formed with a bounding rib A<SP>5</SP> and is faced on both sides with rubber E.
