Table Tennis Racket {PADDLE}
An example relates to a batting racket.
Table tennis is the official Olympic sport and is loved by many people for national sports. To play ping pong or play a game, you need a lot of equipment and tools, the most important of which is the racket.
The importance of the racket will not only improve the individual's ability but also determine the outcome of the important game. Table tennis rackets have a fan holder shape and a shake hand shape. Shake-handed racquets are used by many players worldwide because they consume less health and are more effective in attack and defense than fan holder racquets. Even people who grab hold of existing fan holders switch to shake hands.
However, in a shake hand type racket, you need to hold the racket handle like a handshake and fix it by bending your wrist to hit the ball correctly. At this time, the ping pong balls come and go at a high speed, and in order to neutralize the tremendous opponent's rotation, a high level of skill is required to adjust the angle of the racket at the moment.
Here, in the racket of the shake hand type, there is a difficulty in adjusting the angle in a state of bending the wrist. In addition, in the shake hand type racket, the angle of the racket, which was initially caught, is changed, and the ball cannot be hit as desired.
In addition, in the shake hand type racket, the sense of unity between the hand and the racket is poor.
The embodiment is intended to provide a table tennis racket that can hit the ball more accurately without straining the wrist, and improves the sense of unity with the hand.
Table tennis rackets according to the embodiment includes a plate, a groove penetrating the plate, for inserting a finger and an elastic member disposed on the plate.
The table tennis racket according to the embodiment includes a plate and a groove, and when the finger is held in the groove to hold the plate, the grip of the table tennis racket is formed. At this time, the direction of the table tennis racket is horizontal with the direction of the grip.
Accordingly, even when the table tennis racket according to the embodiment is held without bending the wrist, the direction and the arm of the table tennis racket are straight.
Accordingly, using the ping pong racket according to the embodiment does not strain the wrist and can hit the ball more accurately. In addition, since the finger is inserted into the plate itself, the sense of unity between the table tennis racket and the hand is improved.
1 is a perspective view illustrating a table tennis racket according to an embodiment.
Referring to FIG. 1, the table tennis racket includes a plate 100, a groove 200, a grip auxiliary member 300, an elastic member 400, and a cover member 500.
The plate 100 has an elliptical shape when viewed in a plan view. In more detail, the plate 100 has an elliptical shape in which part thereof is cut when viewed in a plan view. Alternatively, the plate 100 may have a circular planar shape.
The plate 100 has a first side 110 and a second side 120. The first side surface 110 is curved and the second side surface 120 is flat. Examples of materials that may be used as the plate 100 include wood or plastics.
The groove 200 is formed through the plate 100. The groove 200 includes a first groove 210 and a second groove 220.
The first groove 210 is formed in one direction and is formed at an edge when viewed in a plan view. In addition, the first groove 210 is formed in the same direction as the direction in which the second side surface 120 extends. That is, the one direction and the second side surface 120 are substantially parallel. Here, the distance W1 between the first groove 210 and the second side surface 120 is about 2 to 3 cm.
The first groove 210 has protrusions 200a formed therein. By the protrusions 200a, fingers are separately inserted into the first grooves 210, and the fingers are more firmly fixed to the table tennis racket. For example, the protrusions 200a may have a concave surface corresponding to the curved surface of the finger.
The second groove 220 is formed to be separated from the extension line in the one direction from the first groove 210. In more detail, the vertical distance S1 from the edge of the second groove 220 and the extension line in the one direction from the first groove 210 is about 1 to 1.5 cm.
That is, the distance between the second side 120 and the second groove 220 is greater than the distance between the second side 120 and the first groove 210.
The plate 100 is divided into a hitting part 130 and a grip part 140 by the groove 200. That is, the grip part 140 is a plate 100 between the groove 200 and the second side surface 120, and the grip part 140 is a part which is held by hand.
In addition, the striking unit 130 is the remaining plate 100, except for the grip, is a portion hitting the table tennis ball.
The grip auxiliary member 300 is disposed on the plate 100. In more detail, the grip auxiliary member 300 is disposed on the grip part 140. Alternatively, the grip assistant member 300 as well as on the plate 100 may be disposed on the second side surface 120, and may also be disposed below the plate 100.
The grip auxiliary member 300 is softer than the plate 100 and has a large friction force. Accordingly, the grip assistant member 300 improves the unity of the hand and the table tennis racket when the grip unit 140 is held by hand and prevents the hand from slipping.
Examples of the material that can be used as the grip auxiliary member 300 include cork or synthetic resin.
The elastic member 400 is disposed on the plate 100. In more detail, the elastic member 400 is disposed on the striking part 130. Alternatively, the elastic member 400 may also be disposed on the grip unit 140. In addition, the elastic member 400 is also disposed below the plate 100.
The elastic member 400 has elasticity. The elastic member 400 improves the repulsive force of the table tennis racket, when the table tennis ball hit the racket, the table tennis ball bounces strongly.
Examples of the material that can be used as the elastic member 400 may be a rubber resin.
The cover member 500 is disposed on the elastic member 400. The cover member 500 covers the elastic member 400. Since the cover member 500 has a friction force, it can give a higher rotational force to the table tennis ball by the table tennis racket.
A middle finger, ring finger, and a holder of a table tennis racket user are inserted into the first groove 210, and an index finger is inserted into the second groove 220. Then, the user grabs the grip 140 with the palm of the hand, to hold the table tennis racket.
At this time, the user can hold the table tennis racket according to the embodiment as if to hold a pistol. Thus, the user can keep the table tennis racket and the arm in a straight line without bending the wrist. That is, the direction of the table tennis racket and the direction of the arm coincide.
Therefore, the table tennis racket according to the embodiment allows the user to hit the table tennis ball more accurately and strongly without straining the user's wrist. In addition, the sense of unity of the user's hand with the table tennis racket according to the embodiment is improved.
And, the table tennis racket according to the embodiment expands the movable range of the user's wrist, thereby improving the playing performance of the user.
